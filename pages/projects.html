Arduino, Eagle, Onshape, and Fritzing: Non-Contact Range Detection of Ultrasonic Sensor (Fall 2020)

While taking ECE 5 (Introduction to Electrical Engineering) at UCSD, myself and two other people formed a group to create a lab assignment. For this lab, the student is meant to work with a HC-SR04 ultrasonic sensor to write Arduino-C code that will print this general distance the pulse travelled upon meeting an obstacle. In addition, they need to graph it onto Arduino IDE’s serial monitor and continue to build upon the HC-SR04 by integrating three LEDs and a buzzer to signify danger zones. An IR Remote and Receiver were also added as additional components to pause the HC-SR04 sensor. If the danger zone is activated, it can be detected whether the obstacle is an object or a human being through a DHT11 temperature/humidity sensor. This will all compose into our final design: the Ultrasonic Sensor Security System.

Python: Project Euler Problems 3, 6, and 9 (Spring 2020)

Find the difference between the sum of the squares of the first one hundred natural numbers and the square of the sum.

    • Research: Tensile Strength of ABS Plastic Moldings and 3D Printed Samples (Summer 2019)

    While working in the Experimental Mechanics Laboratory of Dr. George Youssef in San Diego State University for 5 weeks, myself, with the help of other members of the lab, created a makeshift aluminum ASTM D638 Standard mold and used an oven to melt plastic in it. We also used the lab's 3D printing technology to create some vertical, horizontal, and flat ASTM D638 samples. We then determined the tensile strength of the samples using a tensile strength testing machine. At the end of the five weeks, I made and presented a slideshow about my project in front of members of the lab. This amazing educational experience gave me a glimpse of the life of a student working in the Experimental Mechanics Laboratory.
